# Reference: Destiny Rescue

destinyrescue.org is the design and structure model for the Favor
rebuild. Approved by leadership.

## What we are copying (in concept, not visually)

- **Full-width visual bands.** Each band has one job. No mixed
  purposes per band.
- **Hero with a partner-as-hero hook.** Their "rescue a child" cost
  framing is the structural model for our Q1 hook.
- **Give page as segmented tiles.** Each donor type has a tile. Lead
  with monthly.
- **Trust signals near the hero, not buried.** ECFA, financials,
  numbers.
- **Story-forward homepage.** Real names, real photos, real outcomes.
- **Strong single primary CTA per band.** No "Learn more" buttons
  diluting the conversion path.

## What we are not copying

- Their visual identity. We have our own brand book.
- Their tone. They are rescue-focused with urgency framing. Our work
  is missionary-equipping, with a longer arc. Tone should reflect
  that.
- Their navigation structure exactly. Ours is PRAY | GIVE | GO |
  IMPACT | ABOUT.

## Things to study before building the homepage

When you (Claude) build the homepage, web-fetch and screenshot the
Destiny Rescue homepage and give page first. Note:

- Band heights and pacing.
- How they handle their hero photo and overlay.
- How the monthly giving moment is staged.
- Typography hierarchy.
- Footer density and trust signal placement.

Save the screenshots to `06-reference/destiny-rescue-screenshots/` so
the rest of the team can reference them.
