# Voice and Tone

The voice on favorintl.org should sound like a confident, biblically
grounded ministry leader who knows exactly what God is doing and
invites you to be part of it. Not a marketing department. Not a
seminary lecture. Not a guilt-trip fundraiser.

## The five rules

1. **Partner-as-hero.** The reader is the protagonist. Favor is the
   trusted guide who knows the terrain. Every page is written from the
   reader's seat.
2. **"You" before "we" on every page.** First sentence after the H1
   should contain "you" or "your". Count it. If it doesn't, rewrite.
3. **One promise per page.** State it in one sentence above the fold.
4. **One ask per page.** Pray, give, or go. Never all three at once.
5. **Speak from inside the faith, not about it.** "Christ is King" is
   a fact we live from, not a position we defend.

## Words and phrases we use

- Partner. Field. Pastor. Missionary. Story. Proof. Eternity. Kingdom.
- Send. Stand with. Pray for. Equip. Train. Reach.
- "Where others will not go."
- "Transformed Hearts Transform Nations."
- Specific country names. Specific people's names. Specific outcomes.

## Words and phrases we never use

- **Generic nonprofit language:** "Lift up", "amplify", "uplift",
  "empower", "give back", "make a difference", "change the world",
  "create impact", "drive impact", "stakeholders", "ecosystem".
- **DEI vocabulary:** "Marginalized", "underrepresented", "equity",
  "lived experience", "voices", "centered", "BIPOC", "decolonize".
- **Therapeutic Christianity:** "Authentic", "vulnerable", "your truth",
  "self-care", "boundaries" (in the therapy sense), "healing journey".
- **Hedging:** "Some believe", "many would say", "it could be argued",
  "perhaps", "maybe".
- **AI tells:** "In today's world", "at the end of the day", "best
  practices", "leverage", "synergy", "robust", "seamless",
  "best-in-class", "world-class".
- **Bait-and-switch:** "X isn't just Y", "X is more than Y".
- **Em-dashes.** Never. Use a period or parentheses.
- **"Just" or "merely" in a minimizing sense.** Strip them.

## Capitalization

- Capitalize: God, Christ, Jesus, the Holy Spirit, the Father, the
  Son, Scripture, the Gospel, the Bible, the Kingdom.
- Lowercase: missionary, pastor, partner, ministry (unless start of
  sentence or part of a title).
- Title case for page titles and H1s. Sentence case for H2 and below.

## Numbers

- Spell out one through nine. Use numerals for 10 and up.
- Round large impact numbers ("More than 1,200 pastors trained" is
  better than "1,247 pastors trained").
- Always cite the source on a financial or impact number ("FY2025
  annual report").

## Calls to action

Approved CTA verbs:

- **Pray:** Pray with us. Get the 30-day guide. Stand in the gap.
- **Give:** Become a Favor Partner. Give once. Fund a pastor.
- **Go:** Travel with Favor. Join a vision trip. Serve from home.

Avoid: Learn more. Click here. Find out. Discover. Explore. These
verbs are dead weight.

## Sample paragraph (good)

> You can stand with a pastor in northern Uganda who walks 14 miles to
> teach a Saturday Bible school. Forty dollars a month keeps him
> equipped, fed, and on the trail. Favor partners have sent more than
> 1,200 pastors into the places no other ministry will go.

## Sample paragraph (bad, do not write)

> In today's rapidly changing world, Favor International is committed
> to empowering local voices and amplifying the work of dedicated
> change-makers across the African continent. Through our holistic,
> community-centered approach, we strive to create sustainable impact
> at scale. Join us on this journey.

Why it's bad: zero specifics, zero people, zero outcomes, six dead
words, "journey", and it could be any nonprofit in the world.

## Tone by section

| Section          | Tone                                              |
|------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Homepage         | Confident, inviting, story-forward                |
| Pray             | Reverent, specific, urgent                        |
| Give             | Clear, transactional, low-friction                |
| Go               | Adventurous, honest about the cost                |
| Impact           | Evidentiary, numbered, photo-driven               |
| About            | Direct, accountable, no humblebrag                |
| Partner portals  | Insider, warm, practical                          |
| Stories          | Narrative, present-tense when possible, photographed |
| Footer           | Legal-clean, ECFA-forward                         |
