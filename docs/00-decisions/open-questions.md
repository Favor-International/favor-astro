## Q1 — Homepage hero framing

**Anchor:** "Homepage is a 3,000-foot view of who Favor is"
**Raised by:** Stephanie Maier

**Question:** Should the homepage focus on who Favor is, or on the
viewer and how they can be part of the mission? Is there a hook
equivalent to Destiny Rescue's *"You can rescue a child for $X a day"*?

**My take (Will):** Partner-as-hero wins. The org-bio framing is what
nonprofits default to, and it kills conversion. Lead with the partner's
action, the cost per impact unit, and the field outcome.

**Decision needed:** What is Favor's equivalent hook?
- Per missionary supported per month?
- Per pastor trained?
- Per village reached?

**Status:** OPEN. Blocks: homepage hero copy.

## Q3 — Team page design treatment

**Raised by:** Stephanie Maier

**Question:** How are staff and board members shown?
- Static cards with bios underneath?
- Hover-to-reveal bios?

**My recommendation:** Static photo + name + role on the index card.
Click opens a dedicated profile page with full bio, country focus, and
prayer requests where appropriate. Avoid hover-only patterns because
they fail on touch devices.

## Q4 — The four ministry pillars

**Anchor:** "four ministry pillars"
**Raised by:** Stephanie Maier

**Question:** What are the four pillars?

**Best guess based on Favor's existing work:**
1. Pastor training (Portable Bible Schools, GIFT Institute)
2. Direct missionary support (indigenous workers in 14 nations)
3. Mercy ministry (orphan care, widows, the persecuted)
4. Evangelism and church planting

**Status:** RESOLVED 2026-05-19. Confirmed by the v2.1 docx sitemap diagram.
The four pillars are:
1. **Evangelism + Discipleship** — PBS, GIFT Leadership Institute, House of Prayer, Favor FM
2. **Education** — Village Learning Centers, Primary + Secondary, Leadership Training
3. **Community Development** — Trauma Counseling, Medical, Church Planting + Construction
4. **Economic Empowerment** — Sustainability, Women's Empowerment, Vocation

Routes use the full pillar slugs (e.g., `/impact/evangelism-discipleship/`).
Logged in `decision-log.md` 2026-05-19.

## Q5 — Pastors vs Churches portal consolidation

**Anchor:** "login-based portals" (For Pastors, For Churches, For
Current Partners)
**Raised by:** Stephanie Maier

**Question:** Do we need separate Pastors and Churches portals, or
merge into one? Stephanie thinks one Pastors portal covers both. Wants
Josh's input.

**My take:** They serve different decision-makers. A senior pastor
exploring how Favor equips him personally is a different conversation

toward keeping them separate but sharing content modules.

## Q7 — Nav real estate and GIVE button placement

**Anchor:** (portal section)
**Raised by:** Stephanie Maier

**Questions:**
1. Should Pastor and Ambassador roles also appear under the PARTNER
   portal entry?
2. Does the GIVE button need to live in the nav, given that GIVE is
   already a primary nav item and the homepage features it heavily?

**My take:**
1. Yes, add Ambassador as a portal type. Pastor already covered. The
   portal dropdown should list: Pastors, Churches (pending Q5),
   Ambassadors, Current Partners.
2. Keep the **GIVE NOW** button. It is the highest-converting placement
   on a nonprofit site. The nav GIVE item and the GIVE NOW button serve
   different intents (browse vs immediate action). Both stay.

**Status:** OPEN. Awaiting Stephanie or Carole confirmation.
